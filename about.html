About Us

Back to Ground Zero (BTGZ) started in 2017 as a pet project by a group of friends who were passionate about sustainable living in Singapore. In collaboration with Ground-Up Initiative, BTGZ hosted a series of mass events aimed to raise awareness on the harmful effects of excessive consumption and the benefits of sustainable living. In particular, Ground Zero Festival 2019 was funded by the Young ChangeMakers (YCM) Grant, and was Singapore's first Kampung-based minimal waste mass event. The festival, which garnered >1K participants, featured local nature photographers, musicians, plant-based chefs, and planet-centric art exhibitions, among others.

Fast forward to today, our work still focuses on bringing people to their natural roots. We design and carry out educational sessions for young kids to develop a deeper appreciation for nature and the environment from the inside out. We believe that through education, we can build motivation organically by connecting the "how" with the "why". Our long term goal is to bridge the information gap about sustainability and mental health resilience among underserved families. Apart from educational programmes, we also give information talks on sustainable living, and run social media collaborations with local partners.
